# Who I am

I build software for robots that need to know where they are. My focus is sensor fusion — taking noisy, unreliable signals from IMUs, GPS, and wheel encoders and producing a single, reliable state estimate your robot can actually navigate from.

I'm the author of FusionCore, a ROS 2 UKF that I built after hitting the same walls with robot_localization too many times: no native ECEF GPS handling, no IMU bias estimation, and noise covariances you have to hand-tune and re-tune.

I benchmark honestly. Six sequences across all four seasons on NCLT — wins and the one loss both reported.

FusionCore

ROS 2 UKF sensor fusion  ·  Apache 2.0  ·  Active

A 22-state Unscented Kalman Filter for mobile robots. Fuses IMU, wheel encoders, and GPS into one position estimate at 100 Hz. No manual noise tuning.

## Why it exists

robot_localization is the default answer for ROS sensor fusion. It works, but it has real gaps: GPS goes through navsat_transform (extra node, added latency, UTM zone boundary issues), no IMU bias estimation, noise covariances you tune by hand.

The designated replacement (fuse) still has incomplete GPS support as of early 2026. So I built FusionCore. ECEF GPS handled natively — no coordinate projection, no extra node. IMU bias estimated online. Noise covariance adapted from the innovation sequence. Every sensor gated with a chi-squared test.

NCLT Benchmark Suite

Reproducible  ·  Open methodology  ·  All seasons

A full pipeline for benchmarking robot localization on the NCLT (University of Michigan North Campus Long-Term Vision and Lidar Dataset). Six sequences across winter, spring, summer, and fall. RTK GPS as ground truth.

## Why it matters

Most sensor fusion benchmarks cherry-pick their best sequence. This one doesn't. All six sequences are reported — including the one FusionCore loses.

The RL comparison uses Mahalanobis gating enabled (chi²(2,0.999) = 13.82 → threshold 3.72). The gate had no effect on any of the six sequences — original comparison was already fair.
